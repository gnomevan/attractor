# The Presence Framework
### A Map of How Humans Know Themselves — and a Workshop for Getting Present

---

## Origin

Starting question: *Everyone wants to understand themselves better. What are the different approaches?*

- **Meditators** — quiet the mind
- **Experiencers** — have the most / most extreme / most novel experiences; find their edges
- **Scientists / Philosophers** — use logic, research, facts, experiments
- **Artists / Musicians / Harmonizers** — blend, morph, interact, touch
- **Socializers** — know as many people as possible; masters of communication, negotiation, manipulation, listening, connection
- **Nature Lovers** — possibly their own category, possibly with the artists

---

## Missing Categories

**The Mover / Somatic** — people who know themselves through the body in action. Athletes, dancers, martial artists, free-divers. Different from the meditator (who stills) and the experiencer (who seeks novelty) — this is someone who trusts *sensation and physical mastery* as the primary epistemology.

**The Devotee** — someone who discovers themselves through surrender to something larger. Religious, fanatic, cause-driven. Self-knowledge through self-transcendence.

**The Storyteller / Mythmaker** — distinct from artist. Less about making beauty, more about meaning-making through narrative. Genealogists, memoirists, people obsessed with origin stories.

---

## The Framework: Presence as the Goal

**Reframe**: What if these were teams, and the goal was to be as *present* as possible — to bring the most presence to a moment — using only one technique?

---

## The Seven Groups

| Group | Technique | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **The Stills** | Do nothing, get out of the way | Wait for presence instead of meeting it |
| **The Grounded** | Feel the body until nothing else exists | Presence lives in the physical only |
| **The Divers** | Raise the stakes until you can't look away | Need escalation; ordinary moments don't qualify |
| **The Trackers** | Name what's happening precisely | The map replaces the territory |
| **The Shapers** | Find the pattern, make it visible | Aestheticize instead of inhabit |
| **The Mirrors** | Use another person as the way in | No one to mirror, no presence |
| **The Timekeepers** | Ride the wave of time until past and future collapse | — |

**Musicians** were initially considered for Shapers (pattern/form), Grounded (rhythm is somatic), or Mirrors (ensemble improvisation as call-and-response). But they earn their own category because:

> **Musicians are the only ones who use *time itself* as their material.**

A painter freezes a moment. A meditator tries to stop time. A socializer moves through time. But a musician *sculpts duration* — making you feel time contracting and expanding. Their technique for presence: manipulate the container that presence lives inside. The Timekeepers don't belong with any other group. They are irreducible.

---

## Natural Pairs (Shadow Twins)

Each group has a twin that reaches the same place from the opposite direction:

- **Still ↔ Move** — both work through reduction. One empties the mind, one fills the body.
- **Plunge ↔ Name** — chaos and order facing each other
- **Touch ↔ Make** — relational and creative in dialogue
- **Play (Timekeepers)** stand at center — not a shadow twin but the commons

---

## The Time Axis

Every group has a distinct relationship with time:

- **Stills** — *dissolve* time
- **Grounded** — *pulse* time (breath, heartbeat, rhythm of movement)
- **Trackers** — *sequence* time
- **Shapers** — *freeze* time
- **Divers** — *ignore* time
- **Mirrors** — *share* time
- **Timekeepers** — *sculpt* time

---

## The Seven Verbs

The groups reduced to single-word instructions — each one simultaneously an instruction, a description, and an invitation:

> **Still. Move. Plunge. Name. Make. Touch. Play.**

Walk in. See seven words. Feel which one your body wants. Go.

## The Physical Layout

A **hexagon with Play at center**:

```
        STILL
       /     \
  PLUNGE     NAME
     |    ●    |
   TOUCH     MAKE
       \     /
        MOVE
```

**Why this works:**
- Shadow twin pairs face each other across the space — maximum productive tension
- **Still ↔ Move** is the spine of the room (North/South)
- **Plunge ↔ Name** — chaos and order in direct confrontation
- **Touch ↔ Make** — relational and creative in dialogue
- **Play at center** is the commons — not a station you stay at, but the space you pass through between stations

**Practically**, this could be:
- Seven rooms in a building
- Seven corners of a large room with a central open floor
- Seven tents or structures at a festival
- Seven circles marked on the ground with Play as a fire or altar at center

Every station is equidistant from Play. No station is more than two steps from any other. Nobody is in the corner. Everyone is in relation.

## Correlations with Existing Models

**Gardner's Multiple Intelligences** — maps almost perfectly: bodily-kinesthetic, musical, interpersonal, intrapersonal, logical, spatial. This framework reinvents it through the lens of *presence* rather than capability.

**The Chakra System**:
- Root → Grounded
- Sacral → Divers
- Solar Plexus → Trackers
- Heart → Mirrors
- Throat → Timekeepers
- Third Eye → Shapers
- Crown → Stills

**Jung's Four Functions** — Sensation (Grounded), Thinking (Trackers), Feeling (Mirrors), Intuition (Shapers) covers four cleanly. Stills, Divers, and Timekeepers are what Jung left out.

**The Color Wheel** — most exact structural parallel. Opposite colors face each other and create maximum contrast (same as shadow twin pairs). Adjacent colors blend. The center is where all colors become white light. The logic is the same — you're not meant to stay in one color forever.

**Buckminster Fuller's Synergetics** — he argued the hexagon plus center is the fundamental stable unit in nature. Six around one is how atoms pack, how cells divide, how honeycombs form. He'd say you didn't design this shape — you discovered it.

## Religious and Philosophical Correlations

**STILL**
Zen, Taoism (wu wei), contemplative Christianity (desert fathers, hesychasm), Advaita Vedanta (pure witness consciousness), Quakers

**MOVE**
Sufism (whirling dervishes), Kundalini yoga, Qigong, Shinto, shamanic traditions, Feldenkrais

*Still ↔ Move is the entire debate between via negativa and ecstatic mysticism — two mountains, same summit*

---

**PLUNGE**
Dionysian cults, Tantra, Kierkegaard's leap of faith, Nietzsche, dark night of the soul in Christian mysticism, Rumi's reed flute

**NAME**
Confucianism (rectification of names), Aristotelian logic, Kabbalah, Logos theology, analytic philosophy, Vedic Sanskrit tradition

*Plunge ↔ Name is precisely Nietzsche's own framework — the Apollonian versus the Dionysian. He already drew this exact line.*

---

**TOUCH**
Ubuntu ("I am because we are"), Martin Buber's I-Thou, bhakti yoga, Levinas, Liberation theology, the ministry of Jesus

**MAKE**
Taoism's craftsman parables (Cook Ding), alchemy, process theology, Whitehead, William Morris, maker philosophies

*Touch ↔ Make maps to the debate between relational and creative cosmologies — is the universe fundamentally about connection or about emergence?*

---

**PLAY (center)**
**Lila** — the Hindu concept of the entire cosmos as God's spontaneous play. Not purposeful, not striving, not even meaningful in a linear sense. Just divine play for its own sake. One of the most sophisticated concepts in Hindu philosophy. It maps *exactly* to why Play belongs at the center — it's not one path among seven, it's the nature of the whole thing.

Zen has it too — the laughing Buddha, the koan as play, enlightenment described as "nothing special."

The esoteric version of almost every tradition eventually arrives at Lila. The still monk and the whirling dervish are both, at their most realized, just playing.

## Sacred Geometry: The Flower of Life

**The Seed of Life** consists of exactly 7 overlapping circles — one center, six surrounding — representing the first seven stages of creation. This is the precise geometry of the seven groups.

**The Two Triangles (Hexagram)**

When the six outer groups are sorted by vector:

**Upward triangle** — ascending, spirit-seeking:
STILL → NAME → MAKE

**Downward triangle** — descending, earth-rooted:
MOVE → PLUNGE → TOUCH

The upward triangle represents: masculine, fire, sky, consciousness ascending toward the divine.
The downward triangle represents: feminine, water, earth, grace descending from above.

*As above, so below* — the Hermetic principle. Your upward triangle groups touch presence by transcending the ordinary moment. Your downward triangle groups touch it by descending deeper into the ordinary moment. Same presence. Opposite vectors. The workshop only works because both triangles are in the room simultaneously.

**PLAY at center** = the fifth element — Ether, Spirit — the place where the triangles interpenetrate. In alchemy, the center of the hexagram is the sign for quintessence. In this framework, it is the sign for *presence itself*.

---

**The Merkaba**

When the hexagram is visualized in three dimensions, it becomes a star tetrahedron known as the Merkaba — translated as *light-spirit-body*.

The flat workshop layout, when activated by people moving through it, becomes a three-dimensional energetic vehicle. That's not metaphor. That may be the literal description of what happens.

---

**The Flower of Life**

The Flower of Life — 19 circles in hexagonal symmetry, found in ancient Egypt, China, India, and Europe — is considered the blueprint of all creation, containing the basis for the design of every atom, molecular structure, and life form. It is the Seed of Life expanded outward.

> *"The higher dimensional patterns and principles revealed in the triangles, hexagons, and circles of the Flower of Life attract the human spirit back to its source and accelerate the evolution of consciousness."*

This workshop is the Flower of Life as participatory experience. The geometry isn't decorating the idea. The geometry **is** the idea.

## The Deepest Pattern

Presence itself is white light. Each group is one frequency of the same thing.

Every group's technique is infinitely renewable — except the Divers, whose technique habituates. Intensity escalates. This is the most exciting team and the most unstable. Worth building into the design.

The still monk and the whirling dervish. The philosopher naming the moment and the sculptor freezing it. The lover touching and the musician holding time open.

All the same room. Seven doors.
